Places to Visit in Kanniyakumari

Kanniyakumari Beach

Kanyakumari is a coastal town in the state of Tamil Nadu on India's southern tip. Jutting into the Laccadive Sea, the town was known as Cape Comorin during British rule and is popular for watching sunrise and sunset over the ocean. It's also a noted pilgrimage site thanks to its Bagavathi Amman Temple, dedicated to a consort of Shiva, and its Our Lady of Ransom Church, a center of Indian Catholicism.

Padmanabhapuram Palace

Located about 15 km from the main town of Kanyakumari, Padmanabhapuram Palace is an ancient wooden palace that was the residence of the rulers of the erstwhile princely state of Travancore. This historical palace, built in the traditional Kerala architectural style, is known for its intricate woodwork, murals, and antique furniture. The palace complex includes a museum displaying artifacts from the royal period.

Suchindram Temple

Situated around 12 km from Kanyakumari, Suchindram is home to the famous Suchindram Temple, dedicated to the Hindu trinity: Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva. The temple is renowned for its towering 134-foot-high gopuram (gateway tower) and its unique musical pillars, which produce different sounds when struck. The temple's architecture is an exquisite blend of Tamil and Kerala styles, and the shrine houses a massive statue of Lord Hanuman.

Thirparappu Waterfalls

Located about 55 km from Kanyakumari, Thirparappu Waterfalls is a popular tourist attraction for nature lovers. The waterfall is surrounded by a lush green landscape, creating a perfect spot for a picnic or a peaceful getaway. The cascading water from a height of about 50 feet is a stunning sight, and visitors can bathe in the pool formed at the base of the waterfall. The area also has a small temple nearby, adding a spiritual touch to the natural beauty.
