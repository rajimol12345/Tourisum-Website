Places to Visit in Kodaikanal

Kodaikanal Lake

One of the most iconic spots in Kodaikanal, Kodaikanal Lake is an artificial lake surrounded by lush greenery and rolling hills. Visitors can enjoy a boat ride on the lake, offering a relaxing way to take in the natural beauty. There are also cycling tracks around the lake, making it a perfect spot for a leisurely stroll or a bike ride. The area is great for picnics and family outings, and it's especially beautiful during sunset.

Coaker's Walk

Coaker's Walk is a paved walkway along the edge of the mountain, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the valleys and hills below. It stretches for about 1 km and is located very close to the Kodaikanal town. The walk is particularly popular for its scenic views of the valley, especially during early mornings when the mist and clouds add to the magical atmosphere. It’s a great place to relax and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

Bryant Park

Located near Kodaikanal Lake, Bryant Park is a well-maintained botanical garden known for its lush greenery and colorful flowerbeds. It covers an area of around 20 acres and houses a wide variety of plants, including both local and exotic species. The park is perfect for a relaxing stroll, and it hosts an annual flower show in May, which attracts tourists from all over. The serene atmosphere of Bryant Park makes it a popular spot for nature lovers.

Pillar Rocks

Pillar Rocks is a famous natural landmark in Kodaikanal, consisting of three giant rock pillars that stand tall, reaching a height of about 400 feet. The sight of these towering rocks amidst the misty valleys is truly spectacular. There’s a viewpoint near the rocks that offers a fantastic view of the surrounding area, and it is a great spot for photography and nature walks. The area is also known for its lush green landscape, making it an ideal place for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.
