Places to Visit in Thanjavur

Brihadeeswarar Temple

The Brihadeeswarar Temple, also known as the Big Temple, is the most iconic landmark in Thanjavur. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a masterpiece of Chola architecture, built by Raja Raja Chola I in the 11th century. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is renowned for its grandeur, including the massive 66-meter tall central dome (the largest of its kind in India). The temple complex also features intricate sculptures, murals, and a massive Nandi (bull) statue. It is a must-visit for history and architecture enthusiasts.

Thanjavur Maratha Palace

The Thanjavur Maratha Palace was the residence of the Maratha rulers of Thanjavur. The palace complex consists of various buildings, including a museum, a durbar hall, and a beautiful garden. The Saraswathi Mahal Library inside the palace is one of the oldest libraries in Asia, with a vast collection of rare manuscripts and ancient texts. Visitors can explore the royal chambers, watchtower, and artifacts that reflect the grandeur of the Maratha dynasty.

Alangudi Temple

Located around 20 km from Thanjavur, the Alangudi Temple is dedicated to Lord Guru (Jupiter), one of the nine planets in Hindu astrology. The temple is an important pilgrimage site for those seeking blessings related to astrology and is particularly visited for Navagraha worship. Saraswathi Mahal Library

The Saraswathi Mahal Library is located within the Thanjavur Maratha Palace and is one of the oldest and most important libraries in India. It houses over 30,000 manuscripts in various languages like Tamil, Sanskrit, Telugu, and Kannada, making it a treasure trove of ancient knowledge. The library is famous for its collection of rare texts, including works on religion, literature, music, and science. The architecture of the library itself is also quite impressive and worth a visit for history and literature lovers.
