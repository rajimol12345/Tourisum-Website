Places to Visit in Rameswaram

Ramanathaswamy Temple

The Ramanathaswamy Temple is the most iconic landmark in Rameswaram and one of the 12 Jyotirlinga shrines dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is known for its magnificent Dravidian architecture, with grand corridors, intricate carvings, and massive gopurams (gateway towers). It is considered one of the holiest pilgrimage sites in India, attracting thousands of devotees every year. The temple also houses the sacred Agni Theertham (holy sea), where pilgrims take a ritual bath before entering the temple.

Dhanushkodi

Located around 20 km from Rameswaram, Dhanushkodi is a beautiful, desolate beach town known for its ruins and breathtaking scenery. It is believed to be the place where Lord Rama built a bridge to Lanka (Sri Lanka) as per Hindu mythology. The beach is a peaceful spot with clear waters and a serene environment. The Dhanushkodi Temple Ruins and the ghost town of Dhanushkodi make it a unique destination. The place is accessible by a 4x4 vehicle and offers stunning views of the Bay of Bengal.

Pamban Bridge

The Pamban Bridge, also known as Rama Setu or Adam's Bridge, is an engineering marvel that connects Rameswaram to the mainland of Tamil Nadu. The bridge offers stunning views of the sea and the surrounding islands. It is particularly famous for its railway section, which is one of the oldest sea bridges in India. Kothandaramar Temple

Located around 12 km from Rameswaram, the Kothandaramar Temple is dedicated to Lord Rama and is situated near the famous Dhanushkodi area. The temple is historically significant as it is believed to be the place where Lord Rama, upon returning from Lanka, performed the final rituals for his vanquished enemies. The temple is surrounded by tranquil waters and offers a calm and peaceful environment, making it an ideal spot for spiritual seekers.
